The Italian Honeymoon

By Rhys & Joanna Dixon

“As soon as I saw you I knew a grand adventure was about to happen.” -A.A. Milne

There is nothing that can be said before you embark on your voyage to Italy together, that will prepare you for the feelings of thrill and joy that you will both experience. The below thread will only display snippets, or highlights, of what was a magical 18 days. Whilst we could go on and on for pages and pages about how perfect Italy is for a honeymoon, we thought it'd be best to create this guide for you adventurers through our own honest, and personal experiences.

The Preparation Stage

Now the majority of people will tell you that preparing for any trip is the hardest part of travelling, and my wife and I are no exception to this. You have a large bucket list of things that you want to do, and you have to try to condense them into a limited number of days. There is also the issue of having to stick to a budget, and often needing to prioritse activities over one another. Do you try to fit in as many places as possibe in order to see as much as you can, or do you travel to a select few places but explore them extensively? Thse are the main issues that we faced when planning our Italy honeymoon. We had a rough budget to stick to, and a set number of days due to work holiday allowance. There was so much of Italy that we wanted to see, and several months would have been the ideal, but we had 18 days to see it all in. In the end, we manged to whittle down the number of stops to 7, with two or three days in each place.
